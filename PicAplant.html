Summary of the project:
As part of our Information Systems Development internship capstone project, we developed a smart application based on a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for image classification. The application utilizes the VGG16 architecture with transfer learning to optimize the image identification process.

The project aims to alleviate the uncertainty associated with encountering unknown plants. Through extensive surveys and interviews with the public, we identified the need for a smart model capable of solving plant classification problems.

Our application offers the following features: Smart AI plant identification Save your identification history and share it with friends Earn points and redeem gifts in the store Connect with others in social forums Take quizzes, earn points, and become an expert

The application is designed to be user-friendly and accessible to anyone interested in learning more about plants. It can be used by botanists, gardeners, hikers, and anyone else who enjoys exploring the natural world.

We believe that our application has the potential to make a significant impact on the field of plant identification. By providing a simple and accurate way to identify plants, we can help people learn more about the natural world and make informed decisions about the plants they encounter.

Here are some additional details about the application:
The CNN model was trained on a dataset of over 14,000,000 images of plants.
The application can identify over 15,000 different species of plants.
The application is available for both Android and iOS devices.
A particularly high percentage of accuracy than the other existing models.
